Product Requirements Document

“Block Drop” — A Play-to-Earn Block-Placement Puzzle on Celo

Draft v1.2 July 20, 2026 Owner: Senior Product Manager

1. Executive Summary

Block Drop is a mobile-first, drag-and-drop block placement puzzle (Block Blast / 1010!-style, not a falling-piece Tetris clone) built on Celo, an Ethereum Layer 2 optimized for low-cost, mobile-friendly payments. Players drag polyomino pieces from a tray onto an 8×8 grid to fill and clear rows and columns, with no rotation, no timer pressure in the core mode, and a game-over state when none of the current pieces fit.

The game combines this session-dense, one-thumb puzzle loop with real-money spending at the moments players already care about—second chance after a loss, tray reshuffles, undo, and other mid-run save tools—plus blitz tournament entry and on-chain “boast” flexing. Player-facing prices are in cUSD (stable), not native CELO—so a Second Chance stays “$0.49” instead of a moving token amount. Gas can still be abstracted in stablecoins. Cosmetic skins are out of v1: players buy another turn, not a new look.

Celo is a strong fit here for the same reasons as the rest of the studio’s slate: it is currently the #1 Ethereum L2 by daily active users, transaction fees average roughly half a cent, users can pay gas in stablecoins instead of a native token, and it already has significant reach in emerging markets via Opera’s MiniPay wallet (16M+ wallets across 65+ countries), a natural acquisition channel for a casual mobile puzzle with a spending loop.

2. Problem Statement & Opportunity

Problem: Block-placement puzzles are among the stickiest genres in mobile (multiple sessions per day, very high D1 retention), which makes them a favorite for aggressive ad-network monetization (frequent interstitials, forced rewarded-video loops) and opaque continue-purchase mechanics. That volume of ad exposure caps user goodwill and margin per session, and players get no ownership of anything they spend on.

Opportunity: Keep the addictive core loop (tray-of-three placement, line clears, combo chains, near-miss board states) but settle the purchases players already make in this genre—continues, reshuffles, undos—directly on-chain in stablecoins, bypassing app-store fees where possible; add provable high-score/combo attestations as a shareable differentiator versus ad-saturated incumbents; and tap Celo’s existing mobile-first, emerging-market user base (MiniPay) as a low-CAC acquisition channel for a genre that already over-indexes on high-frequency, low-friction play.

3. Goals & Success Metrics

Business Goals

  • Launch a free-to-play game with a real-money spending loop generating positive unit economics within 2 quarters of launch.
  • Achieve Day-30 retention ≥ 22% and a paying-user rate ≥ 5% (puzzle-genre benchmarks run higher than arcade: D30 retention 18–28%, payer conversion 3–6%; we lean into proven continue/reshuffle willingness-to-pay rather than cosmetic vanity purchases).
  • Establish Block Drop as a flagship consumer app in the Celo ecosystem (co-marketing potential with Celo Foundation / MiniPay).

Non-Goals (v1)

  • No cosmetic shop / skins / NFT wearables in v1 — players do not buy new looks; they buy another chance to keep playing.
  • No ads — no interstitials, no rewarded video, no ad SDK. Revenue is paid continues/reshuffles and related spends only.
  • No speculative token ($BLOCK token, yield farming) at launch, to avoid gambling/securities classification risk.
  • No player-vs-player wagering of stablecoins in v1 (regulatory complexity); blitz tournaments use a rake/entry-fee model instead, not peer-to-peer betting pools.

4. Target Users & Personas

1. Casual Puzzle Grinder (primary, global) Plays short 2–5 minute sessions many times a day; when the board dies they tap Second Chance or Reshuffle to keep the run alive. May not know or care it’s “on Celo,” the wallet is invisible to them.
2. MiniPay / Emerging-Market Stablecoin User (acquisition wedge) Already has a Celo-based wallet via Opera MiniPay in Africa/LatAm/SE Asia, holds cUSD/USDT, low data-cost sensitivity, values near-zero fees.
3. Crypto-native Competitive Clearer (differentiator audience) Cares about provable high scores and clear-combo streaks, on-chain flex/boast, leaderboard NFTs, blitz tournament prize pools.

5. Core Gameplay (Feature Scope)

5.1 Core Loop (v1 — table stakes, must match Block Blast / 1010! quality bar)

  • 8×8 grid; a tray of 3 polyomino pieces at a time, drag-and-drop placement (no rotation, matching genre convention).
  • Clearing a full row or column removes it; clearing multiple lines in one placement triggers combo scoring.
  • No core-mode timer; game ends when none of the 3 tray pieces fit anywhere on the board.
  • Game modes: Classic (endless, no timer), Blitz (timed sprint for tournament play), Daily Puzzle (fixed seeded board and piece order for fair daily-challenge comparison).
  • Daily challenges & streaks; local + global leaderboards.

5.2 Session Saves (primary monetization — what players actually buy)

  • Second Chance: on game-over (no tray piece fits), pay to revive the run—clear a stuck cell / free space / keep score and continue. Offered at peak regret, when willingness-to-pay is highest.
  • Reshuffle tray: replace the current 3 polyominoes with a new draw when none (or only awkward ones) fit—without ending the run.
  • Undo last move: revert the last placement when a drop ruins the board.
  • Swap / pick a piece: replace one tray piece with a better option mid-run.
  • Optional hard clear (secondary): single-cell bomb or color-match clear as a rarer, higher-priced escape hatch—not the default offer.

5.3 Web3-Native Features (differentiators)

  • Invisible wallet onboarding: wallet created automatically via Magic.link on first login (email/social/phone); player never sees a seed phrase.
  • On-chain “Boast” system: player can pay a small fee to mint an immutable, timestamped attestation of a high score or a clear-combo streak to a smart contract; shareable as a card/link with a verifiable on-chain proof.
  • Tournaments: entry-fee based (cUSD) Blitz-mode cash tournaments scored on lines cleared in a fixed time, with automated smart-contract payout to top finishers, rake taken by the house.
  • Season Pass (optional later): cUSD purchase unlocking bonus continues/reshuffles and challenge rewards over a 4–6 week season—not a cosmetic track.

6.2 Why Session Saves Beat Skins

  • No ads, paid saves only: incumbents flood sessions with rewarded video for a free continue; we skip that entirely and sell Second Chance / Reshuffle as clean paid offers—better UX, cleaner unit economics, no ad-network dependency.
  • Willingness-to-pay is contextual: players buy when they lose a good run, not when browsing a shop. Second Chance and Reshuffle convert at the regret peak; skins convert when players feel optional vanity—far weaker in this genre.
  • Micro price band: helpers at $0.15–$0.19 and first revive at $0.49 maximize conversion (especially MiniPay / emerging markets) while Celo fees stay negligible vs. ticket size.
  • cUSD, not CELO: all player-facing prices are denominated in cUSD (or USDC/USDT). Native CELO is volatile and turns every Undo into a trading decision—wrong for casuals. CELO may appear only as optional under-the-hood gas if ever needed.
  • Same loop incumbents already proved: Block Blast–style titles monetize continues and piece helpers far harder than cosmetics; we meet that demand with cheaper, clearer settlement on Celo.
  • Lower payment-processing cost: Celo fees run near $0.0005–$0.001; stablecoin settlement avoids card interchange (2–3%) when purchases happen via web checkout.
  • Web checkout by default: the game is web-only, so Second Chance / Reshuffle packs settle in-browser on Celo—no app-store IAP cut.
  • Boast-to-acquire loop: a shareable on-chain proof of a high score or long combo streak is viral marketing after a saved run—not after buying a skin.

6.3 Pricing & Economy Guardrails

  • No cosmetic shop in v1; do not dilute the offer surface with skins nobody asked for.
  • Second Chance / Reshuffle / Undo usable only in Classic and Daily modes. Tournament Blitz is continue-free and boost-free so ranked play stays skill-fair and avoids a pay-to-win / skill-gambling classification.
  • Escalate Second Chance within a run: $0.49 → $0.79 → $0.99 (cUSD) so the first continue feels easy and later ones still monetize whales without feeling predatory early.
  • Cap daily spend on continues/tournament entries with soft warnings and optional self-exclusion limits.
  • Jurisdiction / country-by-country legal scoping is out of scope for this PRD; ship as a public web game without a geo launch matrix.

7. Tech Stack Recommendation

7.1 Blockchain Layer — Celo (L2)

Celo migrated from a standalone L1 to a full Ethereum L2 (OP Stack + EigenDA) in March 2025 and is EVM-equivalent, so standard Solidity/Hardhat/Foundry tooling works unmodified.

Fee abstraction lets players pay gas in cUSD/USDC/USDT directly, critical for a “Second Chance for $0.49” tap where you don’t want to explain gas tokens or CELO volatility. Block times of roughly 1 second mean continue purchases and boast-mints confirm fast enough for a mid-run UX loop.

Distribution advantage: MiniPay (Opera) gives access to 16M+ existing Celo wallets in emerging markets, a real acquisition channel and not just an infrastructure choice.

Smart contract stack: Solidity + Foundry (testing/fuzzing) or Hardhat; a simple payment/receipt contract for session-save packs (Second Chance, Reshuffle, Undo); an escrow/attestation contract for Boast; and a tournament-payout contract. No cosmetic ERC-1155 marketplace in v1.

7.2 Wallet & Auth — Magic.link

  • Embedded/dedicated wallet SDK for invisible onboarding: email OTP, SMS, social login, or passkeys; the player logs in like any web2 game and a non-custodial wallet is provisioned behind the scenes (50–100ms wallet creation/signing latency).
  • Non-custodial: Magic’s key-management system means the studio never custodies player funds, reducing regulatory and security burden.
  • Decision: Magic for now. Gas sponsorship / paymaster pairing is a later optimization if needed—not a v1 blocker.

7.3 Blockchain Data / Indexing — Goldsky

  • Goldsky Subgraphs to index on-chain events (session-save purchases, boast attestations, tournament results) into a queryable GraphQL API for the game backend and leaderboard service, sub-second indexing latency, fully managed.
  • Goldsky Mirror to stream the same on-chain events directly into a Postgres/ClickHouse analytics warehouse in real time for BI, fraud detection, and finance reconciliation, with automatic reorg handling.

7.4 Game Client

Decision: web game only. No native app-store build in v1. A drag-and-drop grid puzzle is 2D state + pointer/touch events—no physics engine required.

  • Client: PixiJS or Phaser.js + React, shipped as a responsive web app (PWA optional for “Add to Home Screen”).
  • Players open a URL in the browser (desktop or mobile); purchases settle in that same web session.
  • No Unity, no Capacitor / App Store / Play Store packaging for v1.

7.5 Backend

  • Node.js/TypeScript services (board-state validation, anti-cheat score verification, matchmaking for tournaments), pairs naturally with Goldsky’s GraphQL/webhook outputs and Magic’s SDK (also TS-native).
  • Postgres (via Goldsky Mirror sink) as the source of truth for indexed on-chain data joined with off-chain game-session data.
  • Redis for real-time leaderboard caching and rate limiting.

7.6 Infra / DevOps

  • AWS or GCP for backend hosting; Cloudflare for CDN/edge (asset delivery, DDoS protection).
  • CI/CD: GitHub Actions; contracts tested via Foundry fuzzing plus a third-party audit before mainnet deployment (non-negotiable given real money flows).

8. Security, Compliance & Risk

  • Smart contract audit mandatory before mainnet launch for any contract touching player funds (shop, tournament payouts).
  • KYC/AML: tournament cash payouts above regulatory thresholds may require KYC; plan for a KYC provider integration (many embedded-wallet vendors, including Magic, support pluggable identity/compliance tooling) before scaling tournament stakes.
  • Anti-cheat: server-authoritative board-state validation (client sends placements, server recomputes clears and score) to prevent fraudulent boast/tournament submissions.
  • Regulatory review per country on the tournament entry-fee/rake model (skill-game vs. gambling classification varies globally).
  • Responsible spending: daily/weekly spend caps, cooldowns, and clear odds with no gacha-style randomized paid loot, to reduce regulatory exposure.

10. Decisions Locked

  • Distribution: web game only. Players play in the browser. No Apple/Google app-store build in v1, so no store IAP / checkout-compliance question.
  • Jurisdictions: none in scope for this PRD. No country launch matrix or legal geo-gating work tracked here.
  • Wallets: Magic.link for now. Revisit paymaster / gasless later only if UX needs it.
  • Currency: price and settle shop / continues / tournaments in cUSD (stable). Do not price player offers in native CELO.
  • Price band: Undo/Reshuffle $0.19 · Swap $0.15 · Second Chance $0.49/$0.79/$0.99 · Boast $0.29 · Season Pass $2.99 (all cUSD).
