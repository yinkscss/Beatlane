Product Requirements Document

“Beatlane” — A Four-Lane Rhythm Tap Game on Celo

Draft v1.0 July 20, 2026 Greenfield · replaces Block Drop

1. Executive Summary

Beatlane is a mobile-first, web-playable Piano Tiles–style rhythm tapper: four vertical lanes, black tiles stream toward the player, tap the black tiles in time, miss or tap a wrong lane and the run ends. Speed ramps. Combos and streaks are the dopamine loop. It is not a block-placement puzzle and deliberately avoids competing with that already-taken idea.

The game ships on Celo with MiniPay as a first-class acquisition and payment constraint: players pay continues, song unlocks, and tournament entries in cUSD (not native CELO) with near-zero fees. Wallet onboarding uses Magic.link so casuals never see a seed phrase; MiniPay users can also play with an existing Celo wallet where we support it.

Monetization is impulse and content — Second Chance after a miss, song/track packs, streak Boast attestations, and entry-fee Blitz cups — with no ads and no cosmetic skin shop in v1 (tile themes stay free defaults).

2. Problem Statement & Opportunity

Problem: Classic piano-tile / lane-tap games are among the stickiest short-session mobiles, but most are ad-gating hell (watch to continue, interstitial every fail) or locked inside app-store IAP. Emerging-market players with MiniPay already hold stablecoins and want entertainment that settles for pennies without downloading another opaque APK economy.

Opportunity: Ship the familiar four-lane tap loop on the web, settle continues and song packs in cUSD on Celo, lean into MiniPay’s 16M+ wallet reach, and keep the product clean — no ads, no skin treadmill. Rhythm fail-moments are perfect regret-peak monetization; song packs are real content people pay for (unlike vanity block skins).

Why this isn’t Block Drop: different genre (rhythm tap vs block placement), different skill fantasy (timing/lanes vs spatial packing), different content surface (songs/tracks). Same process, stack, and Celo/MiniPay constraint.

3. Goals & Success Metrics

Business Goals

  • Ship a free-to-play web rhythm game with positive unit economics within 2 quarters of launch.
  • Hit Day-30 retention ≥ 20% and paying-user rate ≥ 5% via continues + song packs.
  • Become a recognizable MiniPay / Celo consumer game (co-marketing ready).

Non-Goals (v1)

  • No ads of any kind.
  • No cosmetic skin marketplace / paid tile skins in v1 (free default look only).
  • No speculative game token or yield farming.
  • No P2P wagering — tournaments use entry fee + house rake only.
  • No native App Store / Play Store clients in v1 (web only).
  • Not a licensed major-label catalog at launch — start with royalty-safe / original / CC tracks.

4. Target Users & Personas

1. Commute Tapper (primary) 1–3 minute sessions, headphones optional, fails at speed, buys Second Chance to beat a personal best. Doesn’t care it’s “crypto.”
2. MiniPay / Emerging-market player (acquisition wedge) Already has cUSD in Opera MiniPay; discovers Beatlane via Celo/MiniPay channels; values tiny fees and mobile web.
3. Streak Flexer (differentiator) Chases multi-hundred combos, mints Boast cards, enters timed Blitz cups for cUSD prizes.

5. Core Gameplay (Feature Scope)

5.1 Core loop (must match Piano Tiles quality bar)

  • Look: soft sky → lavender gradient playfield, faint diamond pattern, four lanes divided by thin white rules, solid black tiles scrolling top → bottom (reference: classic hypercasual piano-tile UI — not a dark “stage” chrome).
  • HUD: top progress rail with star milestones then crown milestones; large centered score; judge bubbles (GREAT / PERFECT / MISS) with light particle sparkles on hit.
  • Four lanes. Tap/click the lane when a black tile crosses the hit band.
  • Rules: tap black = score; miss or wrong lane = fail (Classic). Dual-lane “DOUBLE” rows appear as obstacle events (not every row).
  • Speed curve: gradual ramp plus discrete SPEED UP events (banner + short countdown ring).
  • Modes: Classic · Zen (no fail) · Blitz (timed) · Daily Track (seeded).

5.2 Obstacle challenges (mid-run events)

Charts inject short rule-change events and special tile shapes so Hard runs feel tricky. Banners last ~3–8s; shape obstacles are chart-authored.

Basic events

  • SPEED UP — scroll rate jumps; countdown ring.
  • HOLD — tall tile; press and keep holding until the full length finishes through the hit band (early release ≈ miss; windows can be slightly forgiving).
  • DON'T TAP — bomb tile; tapping fails.
  • ICE / GOLD / FOG / REVERSE — tempo, bonus, visibility, lane-flip modifiers.

Hard / tricky shapes (v1 Hard + late Normal)

  • LONG HOLD — extra-tall tile spanning ~½ screen; release early = miss.
  • STRETCH / BRIDGE — one tile that extends horizontally into an adjacent lane (2-wide bar); must cover both lanes (two fingers or slide).
  • TRIPLE BAR — 3-lane wide bar; rare spike difficulty.
  • L-HOOK — vertical stem in one lane + foot extending into a neighbor; hit stem then hold/slide into the foot.
  • ZIG / STEP — staggered steps across lanes (tap A → B → C in quick sequence as the step descends).
  • SPLIT — two short tiles in outer lanes with empty middle; both must be hit same beat.
  • FAKE GAP — long tile with a white “break” in the middle that must not be tapped (hold only the black segments / re-press after gap).
  • SLIDE — tile that starts in one lane and animates sideways into another before the hit line; player must track the moving lane.
  • CASCADE — rapid back-to-back tiles down a diagonal (1→2→3→4) with almost no gap.
  • TRAP DOUBLE — DOUBLE row where one cell is a bomb; must hit only the safe lane(s).

Tournament Blitz whitelists a fair subset (e.g. Speed Up, Hold, Bridge — no Reverse/Fog/Fake Gap).

5.3 Audio / content

  • Each run plays a backing track + simple tap SFX (perfect / great / miss / UI); mute toggle always available.
  • v1 catalog: 8–12 free original/CC tracks + paid packs (3–5 songs) in cUSD.
  • Each song is a pair: compressed audio file + JSON chart (lane timelines, BPM/offset, note times). Charts are designer-authored Easy / Normal / Hard — not full MIDI pianos.
  • Pack / track unlocks gate which audio+chart pairs the client may fetch.

Audio runtime (locked with stack)

  • Engine: browser Web Audio API only in v1 — no Howler/Tone/extra audio libs unless a later need appears. PixiJS owns visuals; audio is a thin shared module beside the game loop.
  • Graph: one session AudioContext; master → music gain + SFX gain. Mute sets gains to 0 (prefer gain mute over tearing down the context).
  • Music: decode/stream track from Supabase Storage (or CDN); start with the run; playback clock is the sync reference for scrolling tiles.
  • Gameplay clock: tiles and judge windows are driven by the chart + music start time, not by analyzing the waveform. Audio is for feel; the chart is source of truth.
  • SFX: short preloaded buffers as one-shots so they never interrupt the bed track.
  • Competitive fairness: Daily / Blitz re-validate tap timestamps vs chart server-side; clients cannot “win” by desyncing audio.

5.4 Session saves & paid helpers

  • Second Chance: on fail, pay to revive the run and keep score (same speed — no slowdown). Optional short post-revive shield is tunable; escalating price in-run ($0.49 → $0.79 → $0.99 cUSD).
  • Slow-mo (3s): $0.19 — optional panic button, disabled in tournament Blitz.
  • Shield (one miss): $0.29 — Classic/Daily only.

5.5 Web3 differentiators

  • Magic invisible wallet; MiniPay deep-link / “Play with MiniPay” path as first-class CTA on mobile web.
  • Boast: mint on-chain attestation of combo streak or Blitz score ($0.29 cUSD); shareable card.
  • Tournaments: Blitz entry fee, rake, smart-contract payout — boosts off.
  • Season Pass: continues + track unlocks over 4–6 weeks ($2.99) — not cosmetics.

8. Security, Compliance & Risk

  • Contract audit before mainnet for anything holding entry fees / payouts.
  • Server-authoritative score checks for Daily / tournaments (client can lie about combo).
  • Music licensing: only cleared tracks; legal review before branded songs.
  • Responsible spend caps on continues/tournament entries.
  • Jurisdiction matrix still out of scope for this PRD (same prior decision).

10. Decisions Locked

  • Genre: Piano Tiles–style four-lane rhythm tap (Beatlane). Block-placement puzzle abandoned.
  • Distribution: web only.
  • Chain constraint: Celo + MiniPay-first acquisition/payments.
  • Currency: cUSD for all player-facing prices — not CELO.
  • Wallet: Magic.link; MiniPay path supported.
  • No ads; no paid skins in v1.
  • Stack: carried forward (Vite/React/Pixi, Supabase, Vercel, Foundry, etc.).
